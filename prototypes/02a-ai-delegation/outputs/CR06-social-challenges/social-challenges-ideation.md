# Design-Lite Workflow: Social Challenges & Leaderboards

## Step 2: IDEATION

### Evaluation Criteria (Benchmarking Framework)

**Must-Have Criteria (High Weight):**
1. **Social Motivation** (5/5) - Creates genuine social accountability and competition
2. **Technical Feasibility** (5/5) - Can be built with current tech stack in 2-3 sprints
3. **User Privacy** (5/5) - Respects privacy preferences and doesn't expose unwanted data

**Should-Have Criteria (Medium Weight):**
4. **Engagement Impact** (4/5) - Measurable increase in daily active users and retention
5. **Design Consistency** (4/5) - Leverages existing UI patterns and design system
6. **Scalability** (4/5) - Works well with 10 users or 10,000 users

**Nice-to-Have Criteria (Low Weight):**
7. **Competitive Fairness** (3/5) - Prevents power user domination while rewarding achievement
8. **Social Depth** (3/5) - Enables meaningful social interactions beyond just scores
9. **Cross-Platform** (3/5) - Works across mobile, web, and future platforms

### Solution Approaches (4 Options with Cross-Domain Inspiration)

#### Approach A: "Strava-Style Social Challenges"
**Core Concept:** Friend-based challenge creation with real-time progress sharing and celebration mechanics.

**Inspiration:** Strava's social running challenges - users create custom challenges, invite friends, share progress updates, and celebrate milestones together.

**Key Features:**
- Custom challenge creation (e.g., "30-day step challenge" or "Beat my weekly total")
- Friend invitations with accept/decline
- Real-time progress sharing on challenge walls
- Achievement celebrations and social recognition
- Private group challenges vs public leaderboards

**Cross-Domain Inspiration:** Social fitness apps like Strava and Fitbit where social accountability drives 40% higher completion rates.

**Build Effort:** Medium (3-4 weeks)
**Risk:** Friend network adoption - what if users don't have app-using friends?

#### Approach B: "Duolingo-Style Streaks & Leaderboards"
**Core Concept:** Gamified social leaderboards with streak protection and friendly competition.

**Inspiration:** Duolingo's social features - friend leaderboards, streak freezes, and social encouragement that increased engagement by 25%.

**Key Features:**
- Weekly/monthly leaderboards by challenge category
- Streak protection (social "insurance" from friends)
- Encouragement system (cheers, motivational messages)
- Progress visualization with social comparison
- Achievement badges shared with friends

**Cross-Domain Inspiration:** Language learning apps where social accountability turned solitary practice into community-driven habit formation.

**Build Effort:** Low (2-3 weeks)
**Risk:** Competition demotivation - could some users feel discouraged rather than motivated?

#### Approach C: "Fantasy Football-Style Draft & Competition"
**Core Concept:** Draft-based team challenges where users build "teams" of friends for collaborative goals.

**Inspiration:** Fantasy sports where the drafting and team management creates deep engagement through ownership and strategy.

**Key Features:**
- Team formation (draft friends for different challenge roles)
- Collaborative scoring (team total based on individual contributions)
- Strategic elements (trade players, set captain bonuses)
- Live scoring updates and match predictions
- Social trash-talking and celebration features

**Cross-Domain Inspiration:** Fantasy sports communities where the combination of strategy, ownership, and social interaction creates lifelong engagement.

**Build Effort:** High (5-6 weeks)
**Risk:** Complexity overload - might be too gamified for fitness app users?

#### Approach D: "Clubhouse-Style Social Circles"
**Core Concept:** Small, intimate social circles focused on shared challenges and peer support.

**Inspiration:** Clubhouse's drop-in audio rooms adapted to visual challenge sharing - create meaningful connections through shared experiences.

**Key Features:**
- Small challenge circles (3-8 friends max)
- Live progress sharing and encouragement sessions
- Peer support and accountability check-ins
- Shared celebration moments and milestone parties
- Circle-based leaderboards (vs global competition)

**Cross-Domain Inspiration:** Social audio platforms where the intimacy of small groups created deeper, more meaningful connections than large-scale social features.

**Build Effort:** Medium-High (4-5 weeks)
**Risk:** Critical mass problem - need enough engaged friends per circle for it to work?
